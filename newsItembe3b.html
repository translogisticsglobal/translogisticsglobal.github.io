Hurricane Harvey Makes Landfall Along the Central Coast of Texas

Hurricane Harvey is making landfall on the central coast of Texas and is expected to bring category 2-3 winds along with historical rain volumes resulting in flooding and mass power outages. Weather models show the hurricane hovering over southern Texas for as long as four days.

Due to the anticipated effects, most transport modes in and out of the region will be heavily restricted. Major ports such as Corpus Christi, Mansfield, Sargent, Galveston, and Houston are currently in the projected path.

If you have any questions or concern for your cargo in this region, please contact your local TransGroup station.
